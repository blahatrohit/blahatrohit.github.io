Mascot Design for Mozilla Tamilnadu

Mozilla Tamilnadu is a group of Contributors who are passionate about Mozilla related Technologies and products. I was a part of the community and was asked to design and develop the new mascot for the community.

Designing a mascot was something that I had never done before, I took it as challenge as it provided me a opportunity to learn and.

PHASE 1: Understanding the requirement

Requirement from Client .

1. Please write the exact name as you would like to appear in your logo.

MozillaTN or mozilla Tamilnadu.

2. What is your tagline if applicable. Do you want this in the logo?

Doing good is part of our code; use it, depend on the design.

3. Do you have specific images or icons that you wish or do not wish to be in your logo?

Fox Mascot.

4. Do you have any colour preferences or any existing colours you want in your logo? .

nothing specific.

5. What attributes of your business would you like your logo to reflect?

Nothing specific.

6. Where will your new logo be used?

Print, website, posters, stickers, apparels.

7. Do any existing logos appeal to you & why? If so, please provide a link to a picture of each logo.

None.

8. Please supply a brief description of the design you have in mind and any other information that would be useful. .

Minimalistic design, easy ti print, uniqueness, brand recognition.
.

PHASE 2: Research, Gathering Concepts

The process started with the research phase. Pinterest was a very handy tool for the inspiration for the mascot. With the research phase I provided the client with a selection of sketches of different mascots, that I felt resonated with their requirement.


With the feed back in mind, I proceeded with the last design of the Fox.

PHASE 3: Prototyping

Within 2 days of the request, I had completed the first iteration of the design. .


The client then had a different request from the design in his exact words

“Is there anything that could be added to the mascot, that would create an image like, it belongs to tamilnadu?” .

This turned out to be ever more challenging than it sounded at the time.
With a days research and different concepts being shared finally it was decided that the fox should be dressed in as a conventional Tamil bhramin.
