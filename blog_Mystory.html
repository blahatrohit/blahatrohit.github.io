MY STORY

April of 2013 I joined Srm University. While sitting of my counselling I looked at the list of options for branches that I had to choose from. I was interested in electronics engineering as I thought it will provide me the skills and knowledge to make stuff but my father being the self declared savior of my life wanted me to take up software engineering as my subject of study, his logic you will be working in a AC room after you get a job and not in a workshop. As most Indian students I was pushed into a world of unknown, but being a lifetime optimist I took up the challenge and thought of giving the software world a shot my expectations being that I will be developing hi class apps and software’s when I graduate.

When I completed 2 years of my course in software engineering, I like most of my peers was experimenting languages like C, C++ and java. When people ask me which programming language I like the most I say Java due it object oriented nature, and having the flexibility to be used on any operating system, but truth be told I liked java because I don’t have to stare at the life less black and blue Turbo C screens on my computer.
I soon learned that though I understood programming it is not something that I would do for the rest of my life, but I had no way out of that life which I was heading to as I had invested far too much to be there. My only option being force myself to fall in love with programming. It is really important for me to fall in love with what I do I will never prefer it any other way.

Social media was a huge thing and having a good amount of likes on Facebook profile image was the what my age of people used to do. A good profile image was important so I tried to find different ways to add filters to my images to make it better, this was where I was introduced to Photoshop. It was fascinating to see how an application can transform the entire image to make it more expressive.

Photoshop was complicated but I learned few simple things like resizing images. Another craze of the time was sticking printed posters. Posters were generally costly to buy online due to the excessive shipping charges but cheap to print. To make some quick bucks I started supplying my college friends with printed posters. Photoshop was an invaluable tool for this. Word began to spread about the Photoshop guy who supplied posters. All the profits were generally shared between me and my roommate who brought in most of my customers.

A few months down the road, I was introduced to an aspiring entrepreneur who wanted me to design custom posters for his website to sell all over India. As much interested I was to take up the job, I wasn’t sure if I will be able to deliver as I had never designed a poster myself, generally I used to copy stuff from google. Needless to say I took up the job and for the next week I say in hours on youtube gathering the necessary skills to learn photoshop and graphic designing.

By January of 2016 www.blahink.com was launched, with me as the lead designer on team.

Designing is something that I fell in love with and lost all interest in coding. As the seasons for placements came in and recruiters started recruiting all my friends, I had no doubt that I don’t be placed in a top notch company and would end up working for a service based company.

But luck would have it Payoda Technologies came up with a open position for a Ux resource, I dint know what ux was but regardless I applied as they had asked for designers. Looking back the entire process of my interviews and assessments seem unreal, it was when I was giving the assessment for the job that I realized that I had learned a lot in the past two year.

I started of with designing to put up better pictures on facebook never had I imagined that a small incident like that will take me places.

- Steve jobs
